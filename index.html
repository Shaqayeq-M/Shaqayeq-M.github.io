I am Shaghayegh Mahmoudi, currently an M.Sc. student studying Genetics at the Royan Institute, affiliated with the University of Science and Culture. Completing my Bachelor's degree in Cellular and Molecular Biology at the same university ignited a profound passion for neuroscience and its intricate connection to genetics.

Throughout my academic journey, I have engaged in extensive learning opportunities, including seminars, online courses, and internships, to deepen my understanding of the human brain. These experiences have not only kept me abreast of the latest developments in neuroscience but also enhanced my laboratory skills, which are essential for my research endeavors.

Currently, my thesis work is centered on Parkinson's disease, specifically focusing on the development of dopaminergic progenitors and their accelerated differentiation into neurons. This project represents my belief that the integration of genetics and neuroscience is pivotal for unlocking new treatments and understanding of neurodegenerative and neuropsychiatric disorders.

My research interests are Neuropsychiatric Disease, Neurodegenerative Disease, Neurogenetics, Reward Circuit, Sleep Disorder, and learning & Memory.
